VTA Portal — Discussion Points for Samy

Prepared by Jai Anand  ·  June 2026  ·  Portal Review & Next Steps

Five topics to cover in today's call. Each topic has the current situation, a direct question for Samy, and a suggested direction. The goal is to get Samy's input before making any changes — especially since he was testing the portal yesterday and may have his own observations.
1
Funding Approval — How Should This Work?
Decision needed
Current situation
The portal has a "Funding Cycle" record separate from the Cost Estimation. To move a patient to "Funding Approved" status, a Funding Cycle with an approval document must exist. But right now there is no "Add Funding Cycle" button on the patient page — and the create form has no document upload field. So the gate is there in the code but cannot actually be completed through the UI.
Question for Samy
Q1. When you receive the funder's approval email, what do you do with it today? Do you file it in a folder, forward it somewhere, or just keep it in your inbox?

Q2. Would it work for you to come into the portal, find the patient, and click a single "Record Funding Approval" button — which asks you to upload that email/letter and enter the approved amount? Or do you prefer a different flow?
Suggestion
→ Add a prominent "Record Funding Approval" button on the patient page, visible only when status is "Awaiting Funding Approval." It opens a focused form: upload approval document + approved amount + funder reference + date. One action completes everything and moves the patient to "Funding Approved." The approval document is stored as legal proof and visible on the patient's document list.
2
Cost Estimation + Funding Cycle — Simplify Into One?
Decision needed
Current situation
The portal has two separate records: Cost Estimation (the quote you sent) and Funding Cycle (the approval you received). These are always linked — there is no approval without a prior quote. Having two records makes the workflow more complicated than it needs to be.
Question for Samy
Q3. In your current Excel, you have "Fee agreed with referrer" and "Funding agreed Phase 1 / Phase 2 / Phase 3" — these are just two columns on the same row (same phase). Would it make sense for the portal to work the same way — one record per phase, where you first fill in what you quoted, and later fill in what was approved?

Q4. Is there ever a case where an approval comes in that doesn't match a specific cost estimation you sent? (e.g. a funder approves a different amount or different number of sessions than you quoted?)
Suggestion
→ Merge both into an enhanced Cost Estimation record with two halves: Estimated Amount (what you quoted) and Approved Amount (what they confirmed). Balance tracking — invoiced vs remaining — sits on the same record. One record per phase, full picture in one place.
3
Email as the Evidence Trail
Process question
Current situation
In VTA's workflow, nearly every financial event is triggered by an email — approval comes by email, associate invoice arrives by email, payment confirmation arrives by email. The portal currently has an Email Intake screen that links emails to patients, but there is no connection between those emails and the financial records (invoices, approvals). If a funder ever disputes a payment, you would need to go back to your email client to find the proof — the portal cannot answer that question today.
Question for Samy
Q5. When a funder's approval email arrives in operations@, who processes it today — you or Sheeba? And what happens to that email after you've read it?

Q6. If there was ever a dispute with a funder about what was agreed, what document would you currently use as your proof? Is the approval email usually clear enough (i.e. does it state the amount and reference number explicitly)?

Q7. When associates send their invoices, do they always attach a PDF, or is it sometimes just numbers in the email body?
Suggestion
→ Extend Email Intake so that when staff process an email, they can tag it as a financial event — "Funding Approval", "Associate Invoice", "Payment Confirmation." The email is then linked directly to the relevant financial record in the portal. Every invoice and approval would have the originating email one click away. Full audit trail, no digging through inboxes.
4
Associate Invoices — Rate Calculation & Process
Gap in portal
Current situation
Each associate has a session rate and travel rate stored in the portal. But when staff log an associate invoice, the portal does not auto-calculate the expected amount — staff type in all the numbers manually. Also, the associate invoice form currently shows all patients in the dropdown, not just patients assigned to the selected associate.
Question for Samy
Q8. When Kate sends her invoice, does she always charge exactly her agreed session rate × sessions, or do the amounts sometimes vary? (e.g. different rates for different activity types, or negotiated exceptions?).

Q9. Are all associates' rates up to date in the portal settings right now? If not, which ones need updating?

Q10. Does Sheeba typically log associate invoices, or do you do this yourself?
Suggestion
→ When an associate and number of sessions are selected on the invoice form, auto-calculate the expected amount from their rate card. Staff can override if the actual invoice differs. Also filter the patient dropdown to only patients assigned to that associate — reduces errors and speeds up logging.

Open question

He may have spotted issues or had ideas that should be addressed first, or that change the direction of some of the suggestions above. Specific prompts if he needs them
